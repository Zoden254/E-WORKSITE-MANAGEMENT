# WORKSITE-MANAGEMENT-SYSTEM

For anyone who has ever work in a cunstruction site, whether large scale or small scale, he/she can assure you the problem of poor record keeping in these sites. Records are kept in the traditional way of pen and paper. That's not a problem ofcourse at the end records are kept. 

But what if the book get lost, rained on or just anything destructive? Even with the book in a good condition, what happens during payment, the foreman starts looking for records of the unpaid employees page by page, calling name by name, what if he/she forgets to tick a paid employee, or accidentally tick an unpaid employee? Overally alot of time is wasted here. 

In the site store, does the management really know the quantities of each an every object in the store? The records may be kept in the same old style of books but what if the store has upto 50 different objects? They'll have to briefly look for an object to modify its quantity every time it changes.
